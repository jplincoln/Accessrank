WCAG 2.2 EXPLAINED

The standard, in plain English.

WCAG 2.2 is the W3C's web accessibility standard — 86 success criteria across four principles. Level AA is the legal target referenced by ADA guidance, the European Accessibility Act, and Section 508.

Every criterion falls under one of four principles, known as POUR. Hit all four and your store works for every shopper — including the screen reader user, the keyboard-only user, the colorblind user, and Google's crawler.

P
Perceivable
Alt text, captions, color contrast — content users can see or hear.
O
Operable
Full keyboard navigation, no traps, generous tap targets.
U
Understandable
Clear labels, predictable patterns, helpful error messages.
R
Robust
Semantic HTML and ARIA so assistive tech reads it correctly.
AA
Target Level AA. Level A is the floor (and not enough). Level AAA is aspirational and unachievable for most ecommerce content. Every law and settlement points to AA.
